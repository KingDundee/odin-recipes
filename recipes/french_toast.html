Golden Soft French Toast

The Recipe

A champion of breakfast recipes, classic French Toast is sure to dominate the taste buds. An array of finely tuned ingredient combinations and tweaks provide for a sandbox of unique taste and consistency experiences.

This perfect French Toast formula has come about from much experimentation and is almost too good to share!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup half & half
  • 1 loaf bread, (Brioche, Challah, or Country Loaf)
  • 4 tablespoons butter

Steps

Before you Start:

  1. It is advised to cut your loaf into 8 (1/2-inch slices). Slices 1-inch in size do quite well also.
  2. Your slices are MUCH more likely to hold up well if you bake them on wire racks at 300° for 8 minutes on each side. If your bread is stale, this isn't as necessary, but fresh bread has a better taste and can obtain the dry consistency that it needs by baking it.

Making the French Toast:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Heat the honey for 15 seconds in the microwave. Whisk it in a pie pan with the eggs, salt, and half & half.
  3. Place a cooling rack on top of a sheet pan.
  4. Dip each side of the bread into the custard, allowing it to absorb for about 10 seconds, (it will hold up well if it's been baked as noted above.)
  5. Place the bread on the cooling rack for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a large pan over low heat.
  7. Place 2 slices of bread at a time in the pan and cook each side until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes/side.
  8. Remove the bread from the pan and place on the middle rack in the oven for 5 minutes.
  9. Repeat for all slices. Serve immediately with syrup, fruit, and/or whipped cream.
